# Common Obsessions and Compulsions Among People With OCD

## Overview
Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) is characterized by **unwanted obsessive thoughts** and **compulsive behaviors** performed to reduce anxiety related to these obsessions. Obsessions are intrusive, repetitive, and distressing thoughts, images, or urges, while compulsions are behaviors (mental or physical) performed to alleviate the distress caused by obsessions.

---

## Common Types of Obsessions
1. **Contamination:** Fear of germs, viruses, body fluids, dirt, chemicals, or disease.
2. **Fear of harming someone:** Worries about losing control and causing harm to oneself or others.
3. **Responsibility:** Anxiety about accidentally causing harm, e.g., leaving a stove on or spilling water that causes injury.
4. **Magical thinking:** Belief that certain actions, words, or objects influence outcomes superstitiously.
5. **Taboo sexual thoughts:** Unwanted sexual thoughts about children, incest, or aggression.
6. **Moral and religious worry:** Concern about being good, committing sins, blasphemy, or past events affecting morals.
7. **Perfectionism:** Obsessions about symmetry, order, numbers, or fear of losing/forgetting important items.
8. **Identity:** Preoccupation with gender identity or sexual orientation that is intrusive and distressing.
9. **Relationships:** Obsessive doubts or fears in romantic relationships, like partner's love or compatibility.

---

## Common Types of Compulsions
- **Cleaning:** Excessive cleaning of body, home, or clothing.
- **Checking:** Repeatedly checking locks, appliances, or actions to prevent harm.
- **Counting:** Counting objects, words, or actions.
- **Repetition:** Repeating words, phrases, or routine activities.
- **Seeking reassurance:** Requesting confirmation from others to reduce anxiety.
- **Order and symmetry:** Rearranging items for balance or perfection.
- **Doing things in multiples:** Completing actions in specific numerical patterns (odd/even numbers).

> Note: Some compulsions may overlap with other conditions like addiction or hoarding, but OCD compulsions are **driven by distressing obsessions**.

---

## Treatment Approaches
### Self-Help Strategies
- Relaxation, mindfulness, distraction, physical activity, and herbal remedies.

### Psychotherapy
- **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT):** Helps identify and change thought patterns.
- **Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT):** Builds cognitive flexibility and mindfulness skills.
- **Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP):** Gradual exposure to triggers without performing compulsions.

### Medications
- **Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs):** Prozac, Luvox, Paxil, Zoloft.

### Advanced Therapies
- **Neurotherapy:** Deep brain stimulation.
- **Neurosurgery:** Bilateral cingulotomy (last resort).

## Key Takeaways for Therapist Chatbot
- Educate users on **common obsessions and compulsions** to normalize experiences.
- Suggest self-help strategies and mindfulness for managing distress.
- Explain evidence-based therapies like **CBT, ACT, and ERP** for OCD.
- Provide guidance on **when to seek professional help**.
- Normalize experiences of shame or guilt and encourage **compassionate self-awareness**.
