# Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) Overview

## Key Points
- CBT helps **identify and change negative thought patterns** that affect emotions and behaviors.  
- It can treat **anxiety, depression, addiction, OCD, eating disorders, and more**.  
- Combines **cognitive therapy** (changing thought patterns) with **behavior therapy** (changing actions).  
- Focuses on **automatic negative thoughts** and their impact on mood and behavior.

---

## Types of CBT
- **Cognitive Therapy**: Changes distorted or inaccurate thoughts and behaviors.  
- **Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)**: Includes emotional regulation and mindfulness.  
- **Multimodal Therapy**: Addresses seven interconnected modalities: behavior, affect, sensation, imagery, cognition, interpersonal factors, and drug/biological considerations.  
- **Rational Emotive Behavior Therapy (REBT)**: Challenges irrational beliefs and replaces them with rational thinking.

---

## CBT Techniques for Users
1. **Identifying Negative Thoughts**  
   - Learn which thoughts, feelings, and situations contribute to maladaptive behaviors.

2. **Practicing New Skills**  
   - Rehearse coping skills in real-life scenarios, e.g., avoiding relapse triggers in addiction.

3. **Goal-Setting**  
   - Set SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant, Time-based).  
   - Focus on process and progress.

4. **Problem-Solving**  
   - Steps: Identify problem → List solutions → Evaluate → Choose → Implement.

5. **Self-Monitoring**  
   - Track behaviors, symptoms, and thoughts to share with therapist.  
   - Can involve journaling, role-playing, relaxation strategies.

---

## Benefits of CBT
- Helps develop **healthier thought patterns** and coping strategies.  
- Short-term and structured; often **effective within 5–20 sessions**.  
- Can be delivered **online or face-to-face**.  
- Effective for **children, adolescents, and adults** with a variety of mental health issues.  
- Evidence-based: effective for **OCD, PTSD, anxiety, depression, eating disorders, insomnia, and substance use disorders**.

---

## Practical Advice for Chatbot
- Explain **how thoughts influence behavior**.  
- Teach users to **identify and challenge negative thoughts**.  
- Encourage **practice of CBT techniques**: journaling, goal-setting, self-monitoring.  
- Emphasize that **progress is gradual**, and CBT requires **active participation**.  
- Recommend **professional guidance** if needed.  

**Finding Help**:  
- Use directories of certified CBT therapists or online therapy platforms (e.g., Talkspace, BetterHelp).  
- Consider personal preferences for online vs. in-person therapy.  
- Check insurance coverage for CBT sessions.
